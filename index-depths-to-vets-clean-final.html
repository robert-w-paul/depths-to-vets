VA Disability Compensation

Learn about VA disability ratings, compensation rates, and how to maximize your benefits with 2025 rates.

Understanding Disability Ratings

VA disability ratings range from 0% to 100% and are based on how severely your condition affects your ability to function. Ratings are assigned in 10% increments.

  • 10-20%: $175.51 - $346.95 monthly (no dependent allowances)
  • 30-60%: $537.42 - $1,395.93 monthly (with dependent allowances)
  • 70-100%: $1,759.19 - $3,831.30 monthly (with dependent allowances)

VA Math Explained

VA uses "whole person theory" - not simple addition. Each disability affects the remaining efficiency:

  • Start with 100% efficiency
  • Apply largest rating first
  • Subsequent ratings affect remaining efficiency
  • Example: 60% + 40% = 76% combined (rounds to 80%)

Static vs Non-Static Conditions

Static: Permanent conditions unlikely to improve (tinnitus, amputations, stable conditions 5+ years)

Non-Static: Conditions subject to periodic review and potential re-examination

Military Retirement Benefits

Comprehensive guide to military retirement systems, concurrent receipt programs, and special compensation.

Retirement Systems

Final Pay (Pre-1980)

2.5% × years of service × final basic pay

Includes annual COLA adjustments

High-36 (1980-1986)

2.5% × years of service × average highest 36 months

Most common system for current retirees

REDUX (Post-1986)

Option with $30,000 bonus at 15 years

Reduced multiplier until age 62

BRS (Post-2018)

2.0% multiplier + TSP matching

Automatic 1% + up to 4% matching

Concurrent Receipt Programs

CRDP (Concurrent Retirement & Disability Pay)

Eligibility: 20+ years service + 50%+ VA rating

Benefit: Receive both military retirement and VA compensation

Tax Status: Military retirement taxable, VA compensation tax-free

CRSC (Combat-Related Special Compensation)

Eligibility: 10%+ VA rating for combat-related disabilities

Benefit: Tax-free monthly compensation

2025 Update: Full retroactive payments now available

SMC Qualifications

SMC-K ($136.06/month)

  • Loss or loss of use of hand, foot, or eye
  • Loss of reproductive organs
  • Total deafness in both ears
  • Can receive up to 3 separate SMC-K awards

SMC-L through SMC-O

  • SMC-L: Loss of both feet, or one foot plus loss of use of other
  • SMC-M: More severe combinations
  • SMC-N: Multiple severe disabilities
  • SMC-O: Most severe combinations before R-level care

SMC-R Levels

  • SMC-R1: Requires aid and attendance for daily activities
  • SMC-R2: Requires regular care from licensed healthcare professional

TDIU & Permanent & Total (P&T)

Understanding Total Disability Individual Unemployability and Permanent & Total status benefits.

TDIU Eligibility

Schedular Requirements (must meet one):

  • One service-connected disability rated 60% or higher
  • Multiple service-connected disabilities with:
    • Combined rating of 70% or higher
    • At least one condition rated 40% or higher

Unemployability Requirements:

  • Unable to secure or maintain substantially gainful employment
  • Due to service-connected disabilities
  • Income typically cannot exceed Federal poverty threshold

P&T Status Benefits

100% Permanent & Total status provides compensation at the 100% rate ($3,831.30 for veteran alone) plus additional benefits:

  • CHAMPVA: Healthcare coverage for dependents
  • Chapter 35 DEA: Educational assistance for family members
  • Property Tax Exemptions: Varies by state
  • VA Loan Benefits: Funding fee waiver
  • National Parks: Free lifetime access pass

Military Records Request

Before you can claim most benefits, you need your records. Here's how to find them - even if files were destroyed or lost.

DD214

Your DD214 is your proof of military service. You can request a copy from the National Archives: archives.gov/veterans.

Official Military Personnel File (OMPF)

Request your full personnel file from the National Personnel Records Center (NPRC). This file contains your service history, evaluations, and more.

Service Treatment Records (STRs)

If you separated before 2014, your STRs may be at NPRC. For more recent service members, request copies via TRICARE or your branch's medical records portal.

VA Claims File (C-File)

Request your VA claims file by submitting a Privacy Act or FOIA request to the VA. This file contains all documents the VA has related to your claims.

VA Medical Records

Veterans can download their VA medical records through My HealtheVet. Ensure you have a Premium account to access all records.

Awards & Decorations

If you are missing medals or awards, each branch has its own request process. Use form SF-180 or contact your branch's awards office to request replacements.
