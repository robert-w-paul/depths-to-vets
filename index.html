Records Request

Before you can claim most benefits, you need your records. Whether you're missing awards, treatment history, or your DD214, here's how to find it — even if your files were destroyed or lost.

DD214

Your DD214 is your proof of military service. You can request a copy from the National Archives: archives.gov/veterans.

Official Military Personnel File (OMPF)

Request your full personnel file from the National Personnel Records Center (NPRC). This file contains your service history, evaluations, and more.

Service Treatment Records (STRs)

If you separated before 2014, your STRs may be at NPRC. For more recent service members (post-2014), request copies via TRICARE or your branch’s medical records portal.

VA Claims File (C-File)

You can request your VA claims file by submitting a Privacy Act or FOIA request to the VA. This file contains all documents the VA has related to your claims.

VA Medical Records

Veterans can download their VA medical records through My HealtheVet. Ensure you have a Premium account to access all records.

Awards & Decorations

If you are missing medals or awards, each branch has its own request process. Use form SF-180 or contact your branch’s awards office to request replacements.

Legal / JAG Records

For legal records (e.g., courts-martial, investigations), contact the JAG or command that handled the case, or file a FOIA request if necessary.

Guard/Reserve Point Summaries

Retirement point summaries (e.g., NGB Form 23 for National Guard) can be obtained through your unit or your branch’s Reserve/Guard personnel system.

Burned or Lost Records

If your records were destroyed (e.g., the 1973 NPRC fire) or lost, request a reconstructed file. Provide details like unit names and service dates to NPRC so they can search alternative archives.
